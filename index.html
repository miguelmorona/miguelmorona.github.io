About me

I am a math student who loves applying them to everyday life challenges. I value the power of mathematics in such different fields. I am a very curious person and this character led me to study a majoring in economathematics and math modeling. My passion for mathematics grew and I saw its application to cryptography, a field that I always love to learn more.

Before, I graduated with a bachelor of science in Mathematical Engineering at Complutense University of Madrid, 2022 with a honorific mention on the bachelor's thesis. Besides, this thesis was given a prize by the ITEFI and CCN as the best spanish bachelor thesis in "Cryptography and information security" in 2023. After this bachelor, I obtained a master's degree in Mathematical Engineering at Complutense University of Madrid, 2023.

Once I finished my studies in Madrid I decided to start a PhD program at University of Vigo, city where I am living in currently.

Interests

In my cryptography career, I have worked as a research intern at IMDEA Software Institute with Dario Fiore and Ignacio Cascudo. The main topics in the two internships I had with them were about Verifiable Computation and Homomorphic encryption. That experience led me to work with Fernando Pérez and Alberto Pedrouzo in Vigo.

I enjoy playing chess. I play since I am 5 years old and I am happy to have been part of a chess club, CDV Vallecas, for more than 15 years. My interest in chess goes beyond playing. I have taught chess to children in Madrid and I definitely loved doing that.
